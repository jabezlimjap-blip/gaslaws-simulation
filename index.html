Gas Laws & Kinetic Molecular Theory

Kinetic Molecular Theory of Gases

The Kinetic Molecular Theory explains the behavior of gases based on the motion and interactions of their particles. According to this theory:

1. Particle Size

Gas particles are extremely small compared to the distances between them.

2. Constant Motion

Gas particles are in constant, random, straight-line motion.

3. Elastic Collisions

Collisions between gas particles and with container walls are perfectly elastic.

4. No Forces

There are no attractive or repulsive forces between gas particles.

5. Temperature Relationship

The average kinetic energy of gas particles is proportional to the temperature in Kelvin.

Gas Laws Relationship

According to the Ideal Gas Law (PV = nRT):

  • When temperature is constant, pressure and volume are inversely related (Boyle's Law)
  • When pressure is constant, volume and temperature are directly related (Charles's Law)
  • When volume is constant, pressure and temperature are directly related (Gay-Lussac's Law)
